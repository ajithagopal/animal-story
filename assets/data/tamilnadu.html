Agriculture, huge institutional buildings, new roads and quarrying along the forest fringes have blocked the century old migratory routes of elephants leading to conflict all around. Humans retaliate and the death of elephants by electrocution is also a serious cause of concern. Valparai plateau with its plantations and workforce lends itself to be a hotspot and conflict zone. In Nilgiris, the problem of plenty could have forced the big cats to venture out more into their past territories, now colonized by humans, another recipe for conflict. Wild boars constitute another menace in nearly a dozen districts as farmers want them to be shot for destroying their crops. In some districts, even peacocks are frowned upon by the farmers for the destruction to their crops.

Conflict zones

Gudalur in The Nilgiris, Valparai Plateau (Elephants, tigers and leopards), Coimbatore and Hosur Forest Divisions (Raiding crops and human habitations frequently).

Nature of conflict: High and Long term

Reasons for animal deaths

Roads, constructions on corridors; electrocution, country bombs and poisoning.

Major incidents

  • Arikomban capture and release.

  • Rewilding of Rivaldo.

  • Death of a makhna despite capturing and re-releasing it thrice.

  • Three elephants died of avittukai and one electrocuted in Coimbatore.

  • Death of 10 tigers in The Nilgiris in a short span of time.

Efforts taken to mitigate conflict

Wildlife Rescue Centres, AI based warning system in Coimbatore to prevent the elephants hit by trains, use of drones to monitor elephant movement, planning an elephant underpass in Hosur division.
