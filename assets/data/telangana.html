TELANGANA

Since the time of declaration of the Kawal Tiger Reserve in 2012, overwhelming human presence has been a source of human-animal conflict in this region. Absence of undisturbed corridor between the tiger reserves in Maharashtra and Chhattisgarh and the Kawal Tiger Reserve in Telangana has resulted in several deaths of humans as well as tigers. Agriculture, mining and developmental works have had their role in broken stretches of forests, obstructing the migration route of the big cat. Kagaz Nagar and Asifabad divisions of the corridor area remain the hotspots of human-animal conflict in the state, with Khammam taking the next place with high presence of Gaur and other herbivores which keep destroying the crops. Black buck is the most detested animal in Narayanpet division, while wild boar has become a menace for crops in at least 13 districts in the state, along with other herbivores.

Conflict zones

Kagaz Nagar and Asifabad divisions in the corridor area of Kawal Tiger Reserve (Tigers).

Khammam (Bison), Narayanpet (Black Buck), Jagtial (Chital) apart from several districts where wild boar is the pest.

Nature of conflict: Minimal, Long Term.

Reasons for animal deaths

Poaching, electrocution by snares set up by farmers and accidents on national highways

Major incidents

  • Death of two tigers by suspected poisoning in Asifabad district in January, 2024 and two human deaths in 2020 in tiger attacks.

Efforts taken to mitigate conflict

Awareness campaigns in tiger reserves, drives to confiscate snares and traps, increased surveillance, rigorous tiger tracking, and compensation for cattle kills
