UTTAR PRADESH

With the increasing penetration of humans in the forest areas through expanding cultivation, tiger reserves and wildlife sanctuary's decreasing forest area due to illegal possessions, dwindling prey base and overlapping needs have led to an increase in human - animal conflict in U.P., primarily the Terai region bordering Nepal. The reserves are not properly separated by a buffer zone. Dudhwa Tiger Reserve that stretches mainly across the Lakhimpur Kheri and Bahraich districts comprising of the Dudhwa National Park, Kishanpur Wildlife Sanctuary and Katarniaghat Wildlife Sanctuary remains the hotspot of the conflict with the geography also contributing as the northern boundary of the park is flanked by the Mohana River flowing along the Indo-Nepal border while the Southern boundary is formed by the river Suheli. It is difficult for authorities to manage the migration of tigers and elephant herds from Nepal. The increasing instances of human-animal conflict forced U.P. to become the first State to declare death due to a man-animal conflict as a 'State-Declared Disaster', with a compensation of Rs. five lakhs to the deceased's family if the death takes place due to an attack by tiger, elephant and seven other wild animals.

Conflict zones

Paliya, Nighasan, Gola blocks of Lakhimpur Kheri district, Powayan block of Shahjahanpur district, North Kheri, South Kheri and Shahjahanpur forest divisions (Tigers, Elephant, Wild Boars, Leopards, Rhinos). Lalaurikhera, Bisalpur,Puranpur and Marauri blocks in Pilibhit.

Nature of conflict: High and Long term

Reasons for animal deaths

Negligence, electrocution.

Major incidents

  • Death of four tigers within a span of one and a half months in May-June 2023 attributed to the negligence of the forest officials in Dudhwa Tiger Reserve, while a tiger that killed five people in less than 40 days due to developing physical deformities at the Dudhwa Tiger Reserve was shifted to the Nawab Wajid Ali Shah Zoo in Lucknow.

Efforts taken to mitigate conflict

Locals trained as 'Bagh Mitra' (Tiger Friends) who sensitize fellow villagers on pug mark identification, understanding tiger behavior and ways to push them back into forest area.
