KARNATAKA

The construction of reservoir, expansion of plantations, besides the railway line work and mini hydel power projects disturbed the wild elephants in parts of Hassan, Kodagu, Chikkamagaluru and Ramanagar districts.

Around 80-90 wild pachyderms have been roaming in parts of Yeslru, Alur, Belur, Sakaleshpur and Arakalgud ranges of forest in Hassan district and Chikkamagaluru and Mudigere ranges in Chikkamagaluru district. The elephants in groups raid crops and tread into human habitats.

The conflict has intensified since the 1980s, when the Forest Department was forced to take up a large-scale operation to capture and translocate elephants for the first time in recent history. Since then, there have been many incidents of human deaths and the deaths of elephants as well.

Hundreds of people in villages in the affected area are constantly in fear as they may encounter elephants anywhere, at any point in time. Schoolchildren, plantation workers, planters, agricultural labourers, and travellers have suffered. Besides that, there were incidents when wild elephants were electrocuted to death as well.

Conflict zones

The villages on the foothills of the Western Ghats spread over Sakaleshpur, Alur, Belur, (all in Hassan division of forest) Chikkamagaluru and Mudigere (Chikkamagaluru division) taluks, parts of Kodagu district and Ramanagara district form the conflict zone.

Nature of conflict: High and Long term

Reasons for animal deaths

Encroachment on corridors, electric fencing to protect farm fields from elephant raids, speeding trains in elephant crossing areas, electrocution by low-hanging wires, and poisoning of crops.

Major incidents

  • December 2023-Jan 2024 Forest Department carried out an operation to capture and translocate elephants.

  • August 2023,Twelve people have died in Hassan, Kodagu and Chikkamagaluru districts in elephant attacks.

  • December 5, 2023 Arjuna, the tusker that carried golden howdah in Mysuru Dasara celebrations, died during an elephant capture operation. The kumki elephant was attacked by a wild elephant.

  • August 31, 2023 H.H. Venkatesh, an expert in firing tranquilizer darts in elephant capture operations, died in an operation.

  • A tusker was found electrocuted in a farmland in Santhe Kodihalli near Kanakapura in Ramanagar district.

  • June 2023 A person died in an elephant attack at Virupasandra in Ramanagara district.

Efforts taken to mitigate conflict

Forest department constituted Rapid Response Teams involving local youths to avoid conflicts.

Elephants meant for translocation are radio-collared so that they are tracked. Female elephants leading herds are also radio-collared. Forest Dept has introduced an SMS alert system for the public.

Railway barricades are in place to restrict the movement of elephants.
